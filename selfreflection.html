Self Reflection

Lesson learned in this assignment are huge! I have learned that every single website, no matter how professional and famous/recognized it is, has its flaws. Some developers, although having a clear plan for their website, may have not thought of certain things that they might be missing out on. Something that I really learned was also making the website user-friendly so that even if you aren't tech savvy, you have difficulties with certain things or you have a condition such as visual impairment, are deaf or need other special help, the resources for that should also be there, such as a speaker button so that people that can't read really well or are deaf can have the website read aloud to them. That's just one example. Overall, I have also learned that usability testing is very important. Although professional developers and other might test a website, a real user that hasn't seen or touch the website before, should know what the website is about at first glance and should also be able to use the website easily and comfortably. I would definitely clear some stuff up before we say sayonara. I was really confused at first on what exactly this assignment was asking for due to many folders not having information in them, they were just completely empty and said "This folder is empty." Another thing was the fact that I had a few issues with Zoom itself as well. For some weird reason, it had a timer that was counting down, therefore, Zoom calls couldn't be too long. Not too sure what that was all about. I also had another issue when recording Tom's call. Anyways, besides all of that, I loved this assignment. Although it was time-consuming, it was really fun to do. Thank you! I have loved this class and assignment. Thank you Dr. Walters for all of your help and patience!
